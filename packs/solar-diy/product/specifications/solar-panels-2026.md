# Solar Panel Landscape — 2026

<!-- context: section=product, topic=solar-panels-specs, related=system-design-fundamentals,component-selection,battery-systems-2026,inverter-types -->

> **Lead summary:** The residential solar panel market in 2026 is split between premium back-contact panels approaching 25% efficiency (Aiko, Recom, LONGi, Maxeon) and high-volume N-type TOPCon panels at 23-24% (Jinko, Trina, Qcells, REC). Standard residential panels now produce 440-500W. Prices range from ~$2.50/W (value TOPCon) to ~$3.75/W (premium back-contact). Maxeon offers the industry's longest warranty at 40 years.

## Cell Technology Comparison

### Back-contact (IBC / ABC / HPBC)

- All electrical contacts on the rear of the cell — maximizes light absorption on the front
- Highest commercially available efficiency: 24-25%
- More complex manufacturing = higher cost
- Leaders: Aiko (ABC), Maxeon (IBC), LONGi (HPBC)
- Near-gapless cell spacing further improves module-level efficiency

### TOPCon (Tunnel Oxide Passivated Contact)

- Dominant high-volume production platform in 2026
- Efficiency: 23-24% at module level
- Better cost/watt ratio than back-contact
- Most Chinese manufacturers have fully transitioned from P-type PERC to N-type TOPCon
- Leaders: Jinko, Trina, Qcells, REC, Risen, Canadian Solar

### HJT (Heterojunction)

- Combines crystalline and amorphous silicon layers
- Excellent temperature coefficient (performs well in heat)
- Less common than TOPCon in 2026 market — higher manufacturing cost without a clear efficiency lead
- Some manufacturers (Panasonic, REC) use HJT variants

## How to Choose

### Roof space is limited
Go for premium back-contact panels (Aiko, Maxeon, LONGi HPBC) — maximum watts per square foot justifies the higher price when you can't fit many panels.

### Budget is the priority
Value TOPCon panels (Jinko, Trina, Canadian Solar) deliver 90%+ of the performance at 60-70% of the cost. The efficiency difference (23.8% vs 25%) is small in absolute production terms.

### Long-term reliability matters most
Maxeon's 40-year warranty is unmatched. REC and LONGi also have strong warranty programs. Consider the manufacturer's financial stability — a warranty is only as good as the company backing it.

### Hot climate
Look at temperature coefficients. Panels with lower (more negative) temperature coefficients lose less power in heat. HJT panels traditionally excel here, as do some TOPCon designs.

## What to Verify on Any Panel Datasheet

1. **Efficiency** — Module-level, not just cell-level
2. **Temperature coefficients** — For Voc (string sizing) and Pmax (production estimates)
3. **Warranty terms** — Product warranty (defects) vs performance guarantee (degradation) — they're different
4. **Certifications** — UL 1703 or IEC 61215/61730
5. **Degradation rate** — Year 1 and annual; quality panels: <2% year 1, <0.5%/year after
6. **Dimensions and weight** — For roof loading and layout planning

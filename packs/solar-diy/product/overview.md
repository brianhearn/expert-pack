# Solar Technology Reference

> **Lead summary:** This pack is the technology backbone of the solar DIY guide. It covers what solar equipment IS — how panels, inverters, and batteries work; current product specifications and comparisons; NEC code requirements; and troubleshooting. Use this alongside the process pack (`../process/`) when you need technical depth on equipment selection or code compliance.

## What This Pack Covers

### Core Technology
- **Solar panels** — Cell technologies (TOPCon, HJT, back-contact), efficiency ratings, degradation, temperature coefficients, and how to read a datasheet
- **Inverters** — Microinverters, string inverters, and power optimizers: architectures, tradeoffs, major brands (Enphase, SolarEdge, SMA, Fronius, Growatt)
- **Battery systems** — Battery chemistries (LFP vs NMC), capacity vs power rating, depth of discharge, round-trip efficiency, and product comparisons (Tesla Powerwall, Enphase IQ, FranklinWH)

### Code & Standards
- NEC 2020/2023 Article 690 requirements for PV systems
- Rapid shutdown requirements (690.12) and the NEC 2014→2023 evolution
- UL 3741 and its impact on rapid shutdown compliance
- Electrical standards that govern residential solar

### Product Specifications (2026 Market)
- Current panel specs: wattage, efficiency, cell type, warranty, cost-per-watt comparisons
- Battery system specs: capacity, power, surge rating, warranty, chemistry
- Inverter comparisons by topology, brand, and application

### Troubleshooting
- Common installation mistakes and how to avoid them
- Performance diagnostics: production below expectations, monitoring alerts, inverter faults
- Grid interconnection issues

# Planning & Installing Residential Solar

> **Lead summary:** A residential solar project has seven sequential phases spanning 3–9 months. Most projects stall in Phase 2 (design) or Phase 4 (permitting) — not because the work is hard, but because people don't know what to expect. This process pack maps the full journey, surfaces the decisions that matter, and documents the gotchas that surprise first-timers.

## The Seven Phases

| Phase | What Happens | Typical Duration |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1. Site Assessment | Roof evaluation, shading, structural, utility research | 1–3 weeks |
| 2. System Design | Load calculation, sizing, layout, string design | 1–3 weeks |
| 3. Component Selection | Choosing panels, inverter, batteries, racking, BOS | 1–4 weeks |
| 4. Permitting & Utility | Building permits, interconnection application, HOA | 4–16 weeks |
| 5. Installation | Racking, panels, wiring, inverter, battery, grounding | 1–5 days |
| 6. Inspection & Commissioning | Electrical inspection, utility meter swap, activation | 2–8 weeks |
| 7. Operations & Maintenance | Monitoring, cleaning, warranty tracking, degradation | Ongoing |

## The Big Three Decisions

Before you dive into phases, three major decision forks will shape everything else:

1. **DIY vs Contractor** — affects cost, timeline, permit complexity, and warranty access
2. **Inverter Topology** — microinverters vs string vs string+optimizers affects shading tolerance, monitoring granularity, cost, and future flexibility
3. **Grid-Tied vs Hybrid vs Off-Grid** — grid-tied is simplest; hybrid adds battery backup; off-grid is for properties without grid access

These decisions are covered in depth in `decisions/`. Make them early — they affect component selection and system design.

## What Makes This Hard

Solar looks simple: panels on roof, wire to inverter, sell power to grid. In practice:

- **Permitting takes longer than installation.** Most jurisdictions require a separate building permit and utility interconnection agreement. The utility review alone can take 4–12 weeks.
- **String sizing is unforgiving.** Miscalculate your string voltage limits and you'll damage your inverter or create a fire hazard.
- **Roof condition is a prerequisite.** Don't put solar on a 15-year-old roof that needs replacing in 5 years.
- **Utility programs change.** Net metering policies, interconnection fees, and incentive programs vary by utility and change frequently.

# Decisions Index

Three major decision frameworks for residential solar projects. Make these decisions before Phase 3 (Component Selection) — they affect your entire system design.

| File | Decision | Core Tradeoff |
|------|----------|--------------|
| diy-vs-contractor.md | DIY or hire a contractor? | Cost vs. complexity, warranty access, and permitted risk |
| inverter-topology.md | String, optimizer, or microinverter? | Cost vs. shading tolerance, monitoring, and future flexibility |
| grid-tied-vs-hybrid.md | Grid-tied, hybrid with battery, or off-grid? | Simplicity vs. backup capability vs. energy independence |

## Decision Order

These decisions are interdependent:

1. **Grid-tied vs hybrid vs off-grid** first — sets the fundamental system architecture
2. **Inverter topology** next — constrained by your grid configuration choice
3. **DIY vs contractor** last — can be decided anytime but affects permit approach and timeline

## Reversibility

| Decision | Reversibility |
|----------|--------------|
| DIY vs contractor | Fully reversible — you can switch to a contractor at any phase |
| Inverter topology | Expensive to change post-install (replace all microinverters or add optimizers) |
| Grid-tied vs hybrid | Adding battery is relatively easy; removing grid connection is costly |
